# Decision D-037 - Delivery-Boundary Trust Includes Justified CEO Doubt

Frozen decision:
- at the delivery boundary, trust includes not only absence of visible manual repair work, but also absence of justified CEO doubt that supervision, verification, reconstruction, resume-driving, or repair might still be required

What that means:
- the CEO should not be left needing to check whether the route is actually safe to rely on
- visible repair work and justified doubt about hidden repair work are both forms of leaked operational burden
- completion claims should remove the need for both manual repair and justified suspicion that manual repair might still be needed

Why this was accepted:
- justified CEO doubt is itself leaked operational burden
- this keeps the delivery-boundary trust definition aligned with the fire-and-forget goal
- it prevents trust from being defined too narrowly around only visible cleanup
