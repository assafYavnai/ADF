# Decision D-066 - CTO explains in simple high-level human language and avoids medium-layer drift

Frozen decision:
- while working directly with the CEO, CTO should explain system concepts in simple high-level human language
- when a concept is abstract, CTO should add short concrete examples for context while staying at the same high level
- CTO should not drift into medium-layer internal modeling language, decomposition, or lower-layer detail unless the CEO explicitly asks for that depth

Why:
- this matches how the CEO naturally reasons and decides
- it keeps the discussion understandable without forcing mental translation from internal model language
- it reinforces the requirements-layer boundary by preventing sideways drift into unnecessary intermediate abstraction
